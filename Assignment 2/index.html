Suicide Rate Across the World

1979 - 2015

The first known act of a suicidal attempt goes far back towards 600 B.C. Since then, Suicide has become synonymous towards society, with deaths as high as 800,000 per year. Has Suicide Rate stabilised over the years? Following World Mental Health Day, this visualisation aims to explore the change in suicide rates over the course of 3 decades. Factors such as Countries, Age Group, Population and Gender will be the basis of the exploration.

Overall Suicide Rates per 100k population across the World

Firstly, we explore the overall trend of suicide rate across the world. On first glance, the darkened area around Russia suggests its high suicide rate – an average of 32.25 suicides per 100,000 population.

The neighbouring country, Kazakhstan can also be seen having a high suicide rate – around 29 suicides per 100,000 population.

Some notable mentions of countries with high suicide rates include USA ( 13 suicides / 100k population),Australia ( 20 suicides/100k population) and Argentina ( 35 suicides / 100k population).

Average Suicide Rates per 100k population across different Age Groups

Here, we can see that the overall trend of suicide rates remains unchanged from 1979, remaining at around ten suicides/100k population.

Upon further exploration, elderly age groups of age 75 and above have the highest suicide rates, reaching a maximum of 30 suicides/100k population in 1995. The graph has since then shown a decreasing trend.

Younger age groups such as those from 5 – 14 years old are least worrying, with rates stabilizing at less than one suicide / 100k population. The remaining age groups have shown an overall decrease in suicide rates, however not by much as rates remain between 5 – 15 suicides / 100k population.

Top 10 Countries with Highest Suicide Rate per Age Group

Hungary has the highest suicide rate per its population, with the majority of its high rate coming from the 75+ age group – an average of 100 suicides, and the 55 – 74 years age group – an average of 54 suicides.

Lithuania has the highest suicide rate for the 35-54 age group – rates around 59 suicides, while Sri Lanka leads the 25-34 age group with rates of 42 suicides. We can also see a similar trend on the teenager age groups of ages 15 – 24, with Sri Lanka – averaging at 48 suicides per its age group.

The youngest age group have the least rates compared to all, a peak of 2 suicides / it’s population

Gender on its impact towards Suicide Rates

Data for this part has been categorized into intervals of 5 years, and it is apparent that throughout the years, Males tend to have a higher suicide rate when compared to Females.

Throughout 3 decades, there hasn’t been an interval where Females have had a higher suicide rate than Males. Suicide Rates reached an all-time high for Males on the interval 1994-1998, where it recorded an average of 24 suicides, while on that same interval, Females only had an average rate of 6 suicides.

The overall trend of suicides rates on Males has since decreased, however, its values are still relatively high when compared to rates of Female Suicide Rates by around 4x. Females, on the other hand, has kept its overall rate stagnant at an average of 5 suicides throughout 3 decades.
